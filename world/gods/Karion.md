Karion is the god of fertility, freedom, and wandering. He is consequently often the patron of travellers, nomads, thieves, and anyone who uses the roadways. He takes unrepentant joy in seeing and experiencing new things, new places, and new people, and is often invoked at crucial life-changing moments, like departures. Karion is a kind, unjudgmental deity, but in his pursuit for new experience can sometimes forget about the consequences of his actions. Followers of Karion do not believe in settling for what they have, but continually seek new challenges and adventures, often leading to accusations of flightiness or a lack of regard for other people.

He has several symbols: 

- Tat on the back of a moving horse
- Two suns overlapping with each other
- A cresting blue/green wave, often made out of glass to reflect light

Although he is a god who originated on the Prime Material Plane, his worship has been enthusiastically taken up in the Feywild, in particular within the Summer Court. ### Karion’s Origins:

Karion  has an attested life as a mortal. Karion was alive in the Age of Fruitlessness on Verderra and cast wish in order to protect prime material planes from blight and corruption. His overarching phrasing meant that in the fulfilment of his wish, his mortal body was destroyed, and his soul was taken back to the first making of the gods. His worship is, therefore, slightly different from the other gods, because it is the vestiges of his wish that emboldens his followers. As a god, however, Karion could no interfere with his own timeline.
