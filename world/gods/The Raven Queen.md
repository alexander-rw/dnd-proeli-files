# The Raven Queen

**Domains:**

- Death
- Fate
- Winter
- Order

**Aliases:**

- The Watchman
- The Bosun
- The Winter Queen

**Description**

The raven queen appears to most as a tall, lithe humanoid with a soft voice, wearing a long black feathered cloak and wearing a white symmetrical porcelain mask that covers her entire face.

She was worshipped by:

- People in mourning who wanted their deceased loved ones be protected from becoming undead, or to get to a place of safety in their afterlife
- Farmers or other humanoids who wished for winters that were not too harsh

## Funeral Rites

Nearly all humanoids on Proeli, even those that did not directly worship The Raven Queen, would often place a coin on each eyelid. 

```
Come fever or famine, 
come the biting winter cold, 
just put two coins upon my eyelid, 
so I can pay the Bosun’s Toll.
```

Worshippers may instead give other important items, including sentimental items of theirs related to the departed, to try and curry favour with the Raven Queen. 

Bodies are then usually either set on fire, or less commonly, ground to a pulp, to ensure that the body does not come back as an undead zombie, wight, or ghoul. 

Bodies that are set on fire with their items are usually considered to have “passed over” when the body and it’s items have burned to dust. It is considered a sign of bad luck if the coin has kept its shape, which usually means that:

- Funeral pyres or equivalent are set to burn as hot and quick as possible, sometimes having additives included within the wood to ensure that the coin is melted properly
- Silver is usually the coinage that is laid upon the eyelids, as it has the lowest boiling point.

It is not known what she does with the items given to her

## History

Although a complete account of the Raven Queen's motivations and origins was not generally known, it was believed that she was originally a much beloved elf queen from the Feywild or the Material Plane of [Nerath](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Nerath) who witnessed the conflict between Corellon & Lolth. Worried that the dispute might tear the Seldarine asunder, she rallied her followers to perform a powerful ritual in order to become a deity, so she could have the leverage to appeal to the deities' senses. The ritual involved the offering of the queen's followers' souls and magic to add to her own power, enabling her to reach Godhood. The followers, who called themselves shadar-kai, firmly believed that their queen was capable of reunifying the elven pantheon and the sundered elves.
