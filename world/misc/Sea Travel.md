# Sea Travel

## Base Speed

| Vessel | Miles per Day |
|---|---|
| Rowboat / small craft | 15 |
| Keelboat | 25 |
| Sailing ship | 40 |
| Warship | 35 |
| Galley (oared) | 50 |

---

## Speed Modifiers

### Weather

| Condition       | Effect                                                 |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Dead calm       | −10 mi (sailing vessels only; galleys unaffected)      |
| Favourable wind | +10 mi                                                 |
| Strong tailwind | +20 mi; DC 12 Survival or veer off course              |
| Headwind        | −15 mi; strong headwind halts all progress             |
| Squall          | −10 mi today; DC 13 Survival or minor rigging damage   |
| Storm           | Halve speed; DC 15 Survival each hour or take on water |

### Crew & Navigation

| Condition | Effect |
|---|---|
| Skeleton crew (≤50%) | −10 mi |
| Expert crew (proficient, well-rested) | +5 mi |
| Skilled navigator with sea charts | +10 mi |
| No navigator | Roll d4 each day; on a 1, veer off course |
| Navigator has neither charts nor landmarks | Disadvantage on all navigation checks |

### Hull & Cargo

| Condition | Effect |
|---|---|
| Overloaded | −10 mi |
| Damaged hull | −10 mi; crew must bail or lose a further 5 mi |
| Fouled hull (barnacles, neglect) | −5 mi |

### Magic

| Condition | Effect |
|---|---|
| *Control Winds* or equivalent | +20 mi for duration |
| *Gust of Wind* (sustained) | +10 mi |
| Guidance from sea creatures or spirits | Removes navigator disadvantage |

## Daily Encounter Check

Roll a **d20** once per day. In known dangerous waters, roll twice and take the higher.

| d20 | Result |
|---|---|
| 1–7 | **Clear sailing.** Nothing of note. |
| 8–11 | **Weather shift.** d4: 1 dead calm (sailing vessels −10 mi), 2 squall, 3 fog (disadvantage on navigation until noon), 4 favourable wind (+10 mi). |
| 12–14 | **Something on the horizon.** A distant sail, floating wreckage, an unmapped smear of land. No immediate threat — but it's there. |
| 15–17 | **Uneasy water.** Shapes beneath the hull, a vessel running dark, a signal fire on an unknown shore. Nothing happens *yet*. |
| 18–19 | **Encounter.** Roll on the encounter table. |
| 20 | **Encounter, and it has the advantage.** Roll on the encounter table; the threat has already spotted the party. |

---

## Going Off Course

When the party veers off course, roll a **d6**:

| d6 | Result |
|---|---|
| 1 | Arrive at destination 1d4 days late |
| 2–3 | 1d4 × 10 miles off target; navigator corrects within a day with a DC 12 check |
| 4–5 | Stumble across an uncharted feature — isle, reef, or wreck |
| 6 | Truly lost; DC 15 navigation check each morning until reoriented |
