# Corvid Corps

The Royal Corvid Corp stands as one of Hawksreach's most distinctive military institutions. Its origins trace back to Queen Isabella's reign during the Third Shadowspawn War (146-148 AC), when a new type of necrotic creature threatened the empire's existence.

During the darkest days of the war, the Corvids emerged as powerful allies. These magnificent creatures, with their natural metallic armor, proved to be crucial in combating the necrotic forces. Queen Isabella personally negotiated the alliance with these intelligent beings, recognizing their potential as partners rather than mere beasts.

Following the successful conclusion of the war in 148 AC, Queen Isabella formally established the Royal Corvid Corp, cementing the partnership between humans and these extraordinary birds. The Corvids proved invaluable in reconnaissance and aerial combat, their metallic feathers capable of deflecting most conventional weapons.

Over the following decades, the Corp evolved from a wartime alliance into an elite force, with specialized breeding and training facilities established in Hawksreach. The massive rookery in the Summit District was completed in 180 AC, becoming both a military installation and a symbol of the city's unique relationship with these extraordinary creatures.

Today, the Royal Corvid Corp serves as both a military unit and a prestigious institution, with riders selected from all races within Hawksreach. The bond between Corvid and rider is considered sacred, often lasting for life.

## Timeline of the Royal Corvid Corp

**146-148 AC:** Third Shadowspawn War. When necrotic creatures threatened Ilkazan, Queen Isabella formed a crucial alliance with the Corvids, turning the tide of the war.

**148 AC:** Following the war's success, formal establishment of the Royal Corvid Corp under Queen Isabella's decree.

**148-160 AC:** Development period of the Corp, including establishment of training protocols and breeding programs.

**180 AC:** Completion of the massive rookery in the Summit District.
