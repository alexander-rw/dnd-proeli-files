# Forge Dasolys - Tiefling Forging Family

# Inspiration notes

MY TIME HAS COME! There are several dwarven forges that compete for the title of “best” forge in Faerûn, though there is no canonical answer as to which is actually superior to all others. There are five main contenders for the title, each of which I shall address.

The first and most obvious of the four is Gauntlgrym as a result of its Great Forge. Built as the capital of the Northkingdom of Delzoun, elves, Netherese archwizards, and likely some of the most venerable dwarven clans (such as the legendarily talented smiths of Clan Ironstar) came together to harness the power of a trapped Dawn Titan. Given its estimable pedigree and the abilities it is described as having in the novels, it is clearly a forge of great power. That said, it is also by far the youngest of the five contenders and as such likely lacks a degree of ancestral potency as might be utilized elsewhere. The fact that a bumbling heretical fool who can only make a single decent magic item per lifetime has crowned himself king (after getting all the better options killed in a stupid war he was himself largely responsible for) certainly doesn’t help.

The second most obvious contender is of course Gracklstugh, particularly I would say the citadel of Dunglorrin Torune (Overlake Hold), which is both the highest temple and seat of the Church of Laduguer and the residence of the Deepking. As befits the greatest temple of a god of industry and magical craftsmanship, I have no doubt that it hosts forges at least the equal of Gauntlgrym’s Great Forge in potency if not in scale.

The next, and in my opinion more likely, candidate would be the city-state of Iltkazar. The last surviving kingdom of the Empire of Shanatar, Iltkazar’s smiths are famed throughout the Realms for their mastery over metals both mundane and magical. Shanatar at its height was far and away the mightiest of all the dwarven realms (save perhaps for Great Bhaerynden), and the dwarves of Iltkazar were historically among the best and most powerful smiths of the former empire. Particularly, I would think that the Ancient Forge, the city’s temple of Moradin, is likely one of the most powerful forges in Faerûn despite its relatively small size.

The penultimate forge in my mind would likely be the forge located at Blackrock Anvil in the Deep Realm. Though precious little information is given on the Anvil, it is stated that smiths who brave the treacherous journey there “insist its age-old forges yield the best temper and refinement of steel”. The mystery of the location, combined with that bit of commentary, personally lead me to believe it must be rather spectacularly potent. Gold Dwarves don’t risk getting dirty for anything less, after all.

Finally, and to me the only real canonical choice, would be the fortress and temple-city of Thuulurn, the Foundry of Stout Souls. The center of the Church of Moradin on Faerûn, it is the largest and oldest of all the Soulforger’s holy sites. I can only imagine the incredible potency of the magics infused within such a holy site to the most ancient and powerful god of craftsmanship in the Forgotten Realms. As such, Thuulurn is the clear choice to me, though even so I doubt it is too far removed from the other options in power.
