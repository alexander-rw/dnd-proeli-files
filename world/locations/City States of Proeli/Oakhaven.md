# Oakhaven

Population: ~80 (mostly human and dwarf)

**Location:** Situated roughly twelve days' travel north-west of Loudwater, not far from [Bluewood forest](Bluewood%20forest%201dac7339ad0580628b9ff5f3bea95d21.md), Oakhaven lies nestled in a shallow valley where a nearby silverstream trickles down from the foothills. The surrounding landscape is a patchwork of gently rolling fields, interspersed with small copses of oak and ash trees – hence the town's name. A well-worn but unpaved track connects Oakhaven to the larger trade routes further west/south-west, a journey most often undertaken by foot, cart, or sturdy horse.

**Population:** Fewer than 80 souls call Oakhaven home. These are mostly human and dwarven families who have tilled the land here for generations.

**Economy:** Oakhaven's lifeblood is the land. Their two primary crops are hardy **wheat**, used for flour and brewing a light, slightly sweet ale, and plump, versatile **root vegetables** (mostly turnips and parsnips) that store well through the long winters. Small vegetable gardens near the homes supplement their diet, and a few families keep chickens, pigs, or a milk cow. There's no dedicated market square; trade is mostly done through direct bartering or occasional visits from traveling merchants.

**Key Locations:**

- **The Barrel:** This sturdy, single-story building stands at the heart of Oakhaven. By day, it serves as the **meeting hall** for the town elder's gatherings and any communal decisions. A large wooden table dominates the center, surrounded by benches. Come evening, the elder's daughter, Elara (a kind woman in her late 20s with a knack for brewing), clears the table and sets out a few casks of their local ale and perhaps a simple stew. It functions as the town's **only tavern**, a place for locals to share news, company, and a drink. There are no dedicated rooms for rent, but travellers might find a spot to unroll a bedroll by the fire in a pinch, for a small favor or coin. The second floor of the Barrel is also an unused cooper’s building, which the town was known for many years ago.
- **The Granary:** A large, stone-built structure on the northern edge of the settlement, used for storing the harvested wheat. It's usually locked, with the key held by the town elder, Maeve (a weathered woman in her early 60s with a sharp mind and a keen eye).
- **The Root Cellars:** Several smaller, earth and stone mounds dot the southern side of the town, marking the individual family root cellars where their valuable vegetables are stored.
- **Scattered Farmsteads:** The homes of Oakhaven are simple but well-maintained wooden structures with thatched roofs, spread out amongst the fields. Each farmstead has its own small barn and animal pens.
- **Town Apothecary**: Run by [The Burlymores](../../Non%20Player%20Characters%20(NPCs)/The%20Burlymores%201dac7339ad0580699729d48a5090f6e5.md) , the town apothecary is a non magical apothecary and general goods store.
