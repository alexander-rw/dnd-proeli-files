# Gallowmere

A small satellite town ~4 days west of [Loudwater](Loudwater%201dac7339ad0580f3aa78c4ac7c9e3b9f.md) located on the south west of the continent. 

### Gallowmere - Village Overview

**Population**: Approximately 250-300 people.

**Race Demographics**: Predominantly human, with some elves, dwarves, and halflings.

### Village Description

Gallowmere is a small, rundown village nestled on the edge of the River Gallow, with a history rooted in grim justice and more recent acts of compassion. The village has an air of faded grandeur, marked by dilapidated buildings and roads that have seen better days. There’s a pervasive sense of neglect and downtrodden-ness throughout the town.

### Key Locations

**1. Gallowmere Shrine (Small Open Stone Building)**

- **Description**: The shrine is a simple, unadorned stone structure. It is open on one side, with weathered pillars holding up a modest roof. Inside, a small altar stands in the center, surrounded by scattered offerings of dried herbs, coins, and candles. The shrine is not dedicated to any of the major gods.
- **History**: It is said to have once been a place where people would come to offer prayers for the souls of those executed in the town’s notorious gallows.

**2. The Gallows (Former Execution Site, Now Abandoned)**

- **Location**: On the outskirts of the village, overlooking the river, stands the remnants of the gallows - an ancient structure made of weather-beaten wood. The execution platform hasn’t been used for over 200 years, but the memories of public executions are still whispered in the streets.
- **History**: Gallowmere once earned its grim name because it was a place where criminals were hanged in public to serve as a warning. Around 100 AC, the village was ruled by a harsh magistrate, and the gallows were a frequent symbol of justice for those accused of theft or worse. The practice ceased when a new ruler took charge, and the gallows were abandoned. No one is alive who was during those days, and with very little recorded history of the gallows themselves, nothing is really known about what went on back then.

**4. The Old Mill (Near to the River Gallow)**

- **Description**: A once-thriving grain mill now stands idle along the river, its wooden structure rotting and worn by years of neglect. The River Gallow flows calmly by, though the mill is no longer in use, and the village's access to flour and grain has been reduced.
- **River Gallow**: The river is relatively calm and flows steadily from the north. It’s narrow but deep enough to swim in, or just about support a small rowing boat. The water is clean, and relatively slow flowing.

### Village Economy & Agriculture

**Crops**: Primarily grown by the few remaining farmers in the surrounding area, wheat is the main crop in Gallowmere, though yields are lower than in more prosperous regions. Other crops include potatoes, cabbage, carrots, and onions, and willow bark. 

**Economy**: The village struggles to survive, with most people relying on barter or the small amount of trade that trickles in from the river. Few merchants visit, and the village has little to offer beyond its agricultural products. Some citizens still attempt to fish in the river, though fishing has not been as profitable as it once was due to the decline in the mill’s output. 

### The Rusty Pitchfork (Tavern)

- **Description**: The Rusty Pitchfork is a small, unremarkable tavern, its wood walls peeling and the roof sagging in places. It is a favorite spot for the local farmers and laborers to meet after long days of work, though it isn’t particularly clean or welcoming. The drinks are cheap and warm, and the food is simple—mostly stews and bread with occasional bits of dried meat.
- **Notable NPC**:
    - **Gwenna** (Tavernkeeper, Human): A no-nonsense woman in her mid-40s with a northern accent. who runs the Rusty Pitchfork with a firm hand. She inherited the tavern after her husband passed, and while she’s not particularly warm, she’s reliable and has seen nearly everything in the village.

### Village Rumors & Secrets

1. **The Ghost of Gallowmere**: There are rumors that the spirits of those hanged in the village still haunt the old gallows site, and that sometimes, especially on foggy nights, the wailing of the dead can be heard nearby.
2. **The River’s Curse**: It is said that the River Gallow used to run dry during the harshest winters but now flows constantly, even in seasons of drought. Some believe it’s a curse tied to the executions of old, though few openly speak of it.

### Miscellaneous Details

- **Village Atmosphere**: Despite its rundown appearance, there is a quiet, unspoken bond between the villagers. They rely on one another, and there is a strong sense of community. It’s a place where people know their neighbors and share what little they have.
- **Climate**: Temperate climate with moderate rainfall. Winters can be harsh, but the village has managed to survive through the seasons, albeit barely.
