# The Labyrinth of the Wyrm Countess

![[The Labyrinth of the Wyrm Countess.png]]

| **Room #1** | This room was once part of a holy temple, but was desecrated long ago. The altar and icons have been mutilated, and blasphemous living inscriptions crawl along the walls like infernal centipedes. Anyone under the effect of a Bless or similar spell is immune to them, anyone else must make a successful Wisdom save upon entering the room or take 2d6 points of damage. Someone has scrawled "The Circle of Traitors was here" on the south wall. |
| --- | --- |
| **Room #2** | The skeleton of a paladin lies against the north wall. It is still wearing its ruined plate armor, but little else. If anyone approaches it, the paladin's ghost manifests and asks for help. Her holy symbol was taken from her and broken, and she cannot pass into the afterlife until it is returned. The three broken pieces are in rooms #3, #4, and #5. |
| **Room #3** | A company of adventurers lay dying here, bleeding from their wounds. If they are healed, they resume their quarrel about which is the traitor amongst them until blades are again drawn. |
| **Room #4** | A fiendish cult summoned and bound an ice fiend to guard this place. It has broken free of its restraints, and the ruined corpses of the cultists now adorn the walls. At the end of every round of combat, it raises one of the cultists as a skeleton to fight for it. |
| **Room #5** | The tomb of a saint lies here, defiled and warded by three traps of fiendish difficulty. If it is opened, the body of the saint is long gone, but a magical mace of disruption remains. |
