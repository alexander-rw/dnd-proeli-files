# Ilkazan

Ilkazan is one of the four primary nations of this PMP, and is one of the most settled. Under the authority and leadership of the Varian Dynasty, Ilkazan has been a world power for the last 300 years. 

Ilkazan was once a collection of small villages and towns, constantly battling each other for resources. However, these small groups were forged into a singular kingdom under the power of Ultark the Unifier, the founder of the Varian Dynasty. 

Population: 250,000 (60,000 centred in the capital)

Capital city: Hawksreach

Common races: orcs, elves, lizardfolk, dragonborn. 

Exports: timber, crops.

## Economy:

Ilkazan's economy is primarily based on its strong agricultural foundation and extensive trade networks. The fertile lands surrounding the settled towns and villages provide abundant crops, which form the backbone of their economic stability.

As the most land-rich nation among its peers, Ilkazan leverages its vast agricultural territories to maintain significant influence over neighboring countries. The extensive farmlands allow for diverse crop production and livestock raising, giving Ilkazan a powerful advantage in trade negotiations and diplomatic relations. Many neighboring nations depend on Ilkazan's agricultural exports for their food security, creating beneficial but potentially unequal trade relationships.

The nation has developed sophisticated irrigation systems and farming techniques over generations, allowing them to cultivate crops even in less favorable conditions. This agricultural success has enabled Ilkazan to maintain a stable food supply for its growing population and generate substantial surplus for trade. Under the Varian Dynasty's rule, Ilkazan has established well-maintained trade routes connecting its various regions and neighboring nations. The kingdom's unified structure has eliminated internal trade barriers that once existed between the separate villages, leading to increased economic prosperity.

The nation also maintains a strong craftsman tradition, with skilled artisans producing high-quality goods in the urban centers. The combination of agricultural wealth, craftsmanship, and organized trade has helped establish Ilkazan as an economic powerhouse in the region, with its agricultural dominance serving as the foundation of its political and economic influence. This is particularly prevelant in the East of the country, closer to the Drive nexus.

## Political organisation

The modern organization of Ilkazan reflects a unique power-sharing arrangement that emerged from a succession crisis. When it became apparent that the king would have no male heirs, a new administrative system was established around his three daughters. Under this system, local lords maintain control over their respective territories but answer to one of the three royal daughters, who serve as regional administrators.

This three-way division of power has created a stable governance structure while maintaining the unity of the kingdom. Each princess oversees a portion of Ilkazan's territories, managing the lords under their jurisdiction and coordinating with their sisters to ensure consistent rule across the nation. The local lords retain significant autonomy in managing their lands but must align with the broader directives and policies set by their respective princess and the crown.

This system has proven remarkably effective, balancing local autonomy with centralized control, while ensuring that power remains firmly within the Varian Dynasty. The arrangement has also helped prevent potential succession disputes by clearly defining the roles and responsibilities of each royal daughter.

However, whispers in the royal court suggest growing tensions between the three princesses as their father ages. While the current system works under his firm guidance, many wonder if the delicate balance of power can survive his passing. Each princess has been cultivating their own loyal followers among the local lords, and some fear that what was meant to be a solution to succession might instead spark a three-way civil war that could tear Ilkazan apart.

## Military organisation

The unification of Ilkazan under Ultark the Unifier was a period marked by intense conflict and strategic brilliance. Known as the Wars of Unification (10-25 AC), this campaign saw Ultark systematically conquering and incorporating the scattered villages and towns that once dotted the landscape. His military genius lay not just in conquest, but in his ability to turn former enemies into allies, often incorporating local leaders into his growing administrative structure.

The early phase of unification focused on securing the fertile river valleys, where most settlements were concentrated. Ultark's forces demonstrated superior tactical organization, using cavalry units to outmaneuver the smaller tribal forces. The Battle of Three Rivers (12 AC) was particularly decisive, where Ultark's forces defeated a coalition of seven different village-states in a single engagement.

However, the true test of Ilkazan's military might came not from internal conflicts, but from external threats. The Shadowspawn Incursions, as they came to be known, began in 63 AC and continue sporadically to this day. These attacks originate from the cursed lands to the south, where ancient magic has twisted the local fauna and flora into monstrous forms. The Shadowspawn - a collective term for the various aberrations, mutated beasts, and corrupted humanoids - launch periodic assaults on Ilkazan's borders.

The First Shadowspawn War (63-66 AC) caught Ilkazan by surprise, with several southern settlements falling to the horror's onslaught. However, the unified military response proved the wisdom of Ultark's unification. The Battle of the Blighted Pass saw Ilkazan forces successfully contain and repel a major Shadowspawn army, establishing the southern defensive line that still stands today.

Subsequent Shadowspawn Wars (91-93 AC, 138-140 AC, and 202-203 AC) have all followed similar patterns: sudden surges of monstrous activity followed by coordinated military responses. Each incursion has led to improvements in Ilkazan's defensive infrastructure, including the construction of the Chain of Vigilance - a series of fortified outposts along the southern border equipped with magical early warning systems.

The most recent Shadowspawn War (202-203 AC) was notably shorter than previous conflicts, demonstrating the effectiveness of Ilkazan's modern military organization and response protocols. However, military leaders warn against complacency, as recent scout reports suggest unusual activity in the cursed lands that might herald another incursion.

This constant threat from the south has significantly influenced Ilkazan's military structure, requiring both standing armies and rapid response forces. The military maintains specialized units trained specifically in combating Shadowspawn threats, including mage-knights who combine martial prowess with magical expertise necessary for dealing with corrupted creatures.

## Timeline

**0 AC:** The Comet appears, marking the beginning of the modern calendar. The Magocracy engineered this collective use of a new calendar because it was getting annoying having different dating systems.

**8 AC:** Ultark the Unifier begins his campaign to unite the scattered villages and towns of what would become Ilkazan.

**18-33 AC:** The Wars of Unification. Ultark systematically conquers and incorporates scattered settlements, establishing the foundations of Ilkazan.

**20 AC:** Battle of Three Rivers - Ultark's forces defeat a coalition of seven village-states in a decisive victory.

**33-48 AC:** Ultark 'The Founder' establishes the Varian Dynasty after unifying the realm. (15-year reign).

**48-93 AC:** King Sebastian 'The Builder' rules for 45 years, establishing major infrastructure and the capital city.

**71-74 AC:** First Shadowspawn War. Southern settlements fall initially, but Ilkazan forces eventually triumph at the Battle of the Blighted Pass.

**85-86 AC:** The Miners' Rebellion - A significant uprising occurs when mining guilds protest against harsh working conditions. Sebastian responds by implementing worker protection laws and establishing the Engineers' Exchange to improve mining safety.

**93-128 AC:** King Aldric 'The Conqueror' rules for 35 years, expanding the kingdom's borders through military campaigns. He also was credited with organising the southern boarder with the Spawnlands, making the country more resilient to these incursions.

**99-101 AC:** Second Shadowspawn War.

**128-168 AC:** Queen Isabella 'The Just' rules for 40 years, establishing the laws of succession and the Royal Court system.

**146-148 AC:** Third Shadowspawn War. This war was one of the most dangerous, as a new type of creature - charged with necrotic light - took defenders by surprise. The country was saved only by the intervention of giant Corvids, who made an alliance with Isabella.

**168-218 AC:** King Magnus 'The Wise' rules for 50 years, ushering in a golden age of prosperity

**210-211 AC:** Fourth Shadowspawn War - Notable for being shorter than previous conflicts, demonstrating Ilkazan's improved military capabilities.

**218-245 AC:** Period of Queen Elowen's influence and prophecies.

**245-Present (333 AC):** King Eldred 'The Aged' rules, facing succession challenges with three daughters.

**~333 AC (Present Day):** Ilkazan faces potential succession challenges with the king's three daughters serving as regional administrators. The nation maintains its position as an agricultural and economic power while remaining vigilant against Shadowspawn threats.
