# The Drowned Arena

The fighting pits of [**Durgam**](Durgam%2019dc7339ad05809db139c0d9aca0f7cc.md) , known as the Drowned Arena, is an area known to few in the city.

The entrance lies in a submerged grotto: Jagged stone teeth line the cavern walls, and bioluminescent barnacles light the way for those who know the sequence to signal passage. Only those who swim through the carved archway—depicting twin krakens in battle—and knock three times on the stone door will see it open, revealing an air pocketed cave system with concealed chambers inside. It is located on the second levl of hte hua shan.

Dimly lit with whale-oil lanterns and glowing fungus, the *Drowned Arena* is where warriors, spellcasters, and desperate souls clash for coin, pride, and blood.

The Drowned Arena is run by [Kai Jun](../Non%20Player%20Characters%20(NPCs)/Kai%20Jun%201efc7339ad05807ab0a8dbc1ce23cb03.md). They are firm, fair, but ruthless. Cheaters are given to the anemone cage to be eaten. Winners are celebrated.

## Three Areas of Fighting

### **1. The Sapphire Lung (Underwater Arena)**

- A wide, circular pit completely submerged in a deep well of water fed by volcanic fissures.
- Chains hang from coral-covered stone columns, giving grapplers something to anchor to.
- Spectators watch through thick glass windows enchanted for pressure and clarity.
- Fights include breath-holding duels, underwater spell duels, and brutal trident skirmishes.
- Magical waterbreathing (included for fighters) is *disabled* mid-fight if the crowd votes it.

### 2. **The Blood Nest (Cavern Arena)**

- Located in a dry chamber just past the underwater tunnel system.
- Cracked basalt floor, jagged ledges, and a sunken dais where “challengers” are thrown into combat. There is a long walkway to the pit
- Audience members above throw coins, curses, and advice from raised scaffolding.
- Popular for monster-vs-gladiator showdowns, as well as regular Dhuhul fights.
- Blood is drained through stone channels and recycled for rituals by the house mages.

### 3. **The Dragon's Spine (Balance Arena)**

- Two thin basalt columns rise 20 feet from the cavern floor, each only about 1 foot apart - barely wide enough to plant a boot. They are 5 feet apart.
- Combatants must balance and battle on the poles, avoiding falling off.
- Fights here can be determined to also be “first to fall off”, a rule specific to the arena

## Three Tiers of Fight

The fights in the Drowned Arena may be fought to three levels:

- **Rakt:** Often done only for show or to prove a point, first blood battles are the least common. The winner gets one gold, and the wagers for fights like this are only every between observers.
- **Athmara:** With a 1.1-1.4x wager multiplier, this fight tier is the most common. First to be knocked unconscious wins. Each combatant is given a special herbal sublimate that will stabilise their enemy; anyone who does not stabilise them immediately forfeits any winnings and in some cases, may be sentenced to immediate death where they stand.
- **No Quarter:** With multipliers from 1.5x all the way up to 10x in some situations, No Quarter fights are to the death. These fights are usually done to settle debts or “legally” kill nemeses, or sometimes by extremely desperate people who need to win it big.

For “Unconcious” and “No Quarter” Tiers, people can wager bets with the house. 

## Rules of Engagement

- Each party may rules for a fight, such as “No shields” or “Slashing implements only”. The most common of these is “No magic cast before the fight affecting combatants”, which generally means that any magic cast beforehand must be dispelled or removed before the fight starts. Another common rule is “Fists only”.
