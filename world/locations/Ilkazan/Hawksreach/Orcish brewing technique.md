# Orcish brewing technique

### **Orcish Brewing Technique: *Bloodfire Fermentation***

**Origin:**

Passed down through generations of warclans, *Bloodfire Fermentation* is a revered orcish brewing technique said to be gifted by fire spirits. Legend tells that the first orc chieftain to tame flame without fear brewed the initial batch in a cracked skull of a fire giant, steeped in the blood of a slain wyvern.

---

### **The Ritual Process:**

### **1. The Blood Oath**

Before brewing begins, the brewer (known as a *Gor’tosh*, or "fire-binder") must offer a blood sacrifice — usually their own, to demonstrate strength and dedication. This is poured into the *Vok’gul*, a ceremonial cauldron forged from melted weapons or armor.

### **2. The Flame Pit**

The brew must be prepared over an Unctoflame, a fire ignited using the fat of a fallen beast and kept alight with powdered bone ash. The flame must burn for three nights without flickering — a test of endurance for the person tasked with maintaining it. It’s believed to impart flavour and body to the brew.

### **3. Ingredients of Worth**

The base is a mash of bloodroot, black barley, wild yeast, and fresh animal blood (ideally from a worthy kill - such as a dire boar or giant stag) is added after fermentation begins. This gives the drink its crimson hue and a deep, metallic bite.

### **4. Fermentation in Spirit-Hollowed Vessels**

The fermented mash is stored in **hollowed-out tree trunks** from the Whispering Forest (or any spiritually significant location). The insides are charred with clan sigils and filled with riverstones collected during a full moon — believed to ward off weak spirits and draw in those with strength. Fermentation takes **six moons** and is only considered complete when the brew howls under the moonlight (literally — the escaping gases through special reed stoppers emit a wailing sound as they release pressure).

---

### **Tasting Notes:**

- Smell: Iron-rich, smoky, and herbaceous.
- Taste: Thick, bitter, with a strong afterburn — like smoked jerky dipped in rootbeer and blood.
