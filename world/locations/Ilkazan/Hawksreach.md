# Hawksreach

Hawksreach is the capital city of Ilkazan, which occupies a natural concave space between two of the peaks in the Alkerst Mountain range. The city is also famous as the roosting ground for the semi-mythic animals the Corvids, giant raven-like birds with natural metallic like armour which the Royal Flying Corp have semi-domesticated.

Originally, the city was a small mountain settlement where Ultark grew up. His son, Sebastian, oversaw the change from the small village into a capital deserving of its empire.

## Population and Society

The capital has a population of approximately 60,000 people, many of whom are involved in intense mining operations for which the city is famous. Hawksreach is notably diverse, with a strong presence of orcs and half-orcs who hold positions of respect throughout the city's hierarchy. Many serve as master smiths, engineers, and military leaders, their natural strength and endurance highly valued in both mining operations and city defense. The orcish influence is particularly visible in the city's architecture, which combines dwarven precision with orcish monumentality.

The city's population also includes significant numbers of dwarves, humans, and mountain gnomes, creating a unique cultural blend. Social status is primarily determined by skill and contribution to the city's industries rather than race or heritage.

## The Royal Corvid Corp

The Royal Corvid Corp stands as one of Hawksreach's most distinctive military institutions. Its origins trace back to Queen Isabella's reign during the Third Shadowspawn War (146-148 AC), when a new type of necrotic creature threatened the empire's existence.

During the darkest days of the war, the Corvids emerged as powerful allies. These magnificent creatures, with their natural metallic armor, proved to be crucial in combating the necrotic forces. Queen Isabella personally negotiated the alliance with these intelligent beings, recognizing their potential as partners rather than mere beasts.

Following the successful conclusion of the war in 148 AC, Queen Isabella formally established the Royal Corvid Corp, cementing the partnership between humans and these extraordinary birds. The Corvids proved invaluable in reconnaissance and aerial combat, their metallic feathers capable of deflecting most conventional weapons.

Over the following decades, the Corp evolved from a wartime alliance into an elite force, with specialized breeding and training facilities established in Hawksreach. The massive rookery in the Summit District was completed in 180 AC, becoming both a military installation and a symbol of the city's unique relationship with these extraordinary creatures.

Today, the Royal Corvid Corp serves as both a military unit and a prestigious institution, with riders selected from all races within Hawksreach. The bond between Corvid and rider is considered sacred, often lasting for life.

## Districts and Notable Locations

Hawksreach's unique geography has shaped its development into distinct districts, each with its own character and purpose:

### The Summit District (The Crown)

Perched at the highest point of the city, the Summit District is a marvel of architectural integration with the mountain itself. The Royal Palace, known as the Eagle's Nest, features sweeping spires and intricate stonework that seems to grow naturally from the peak. The district houses:

- The Royal Corvid Rookery - A massive structure of steel and stone where the Flying Corps trains and houses their legendary birds. The rookery's distinctive metallic domes reflect sunlight across the city.
- The High Court - Where the city's most important legal and political decisions are made, featuring chambers carved directly into the mountain face.
- Noble Estates - Elegant mansions with private gardens maintained through complex irrigation systems.

### Temple of the Still Mirror

At the heart of a natural cavern deep beneath Ilkazan lies the Temple of the Still Mirror, a structure of breath-taking symmetry built around a pool of perfectly motionless, ink-black liquid. The air is perpetually cool and hushed, the silence broken only by the occasional drip of water from stalactites high above—a sound that never reaches the pool itself. This viscous substance, dark as void-glass and impossibly dense, defies all attempts to disturb it. No object cast upon it will float or sink; no spell, blade, or force has marred its surface. Even the cavern stone within arm’s reach of the pool resists all forms of alteration, as though the presence of the liquid sanctifies not only itself but its surroundings.

The temple, built during the reign of King Magnus, is an architectural marvel of balance and repetition—columns arranged in mathematically perfect sequences, floors inlaid with fractal mosaics of silver and obsidian, and walkways designed to echo the geometry of the pool. Every element serves to mirror the principles the pool represents: order, stasis, and the unyielding truth of permanence. Pilgrims come in silence, some from across the continent, to stand before the liquid and see themselves—not in reflection, for the pool gives none—but in contrast. They pray not for change, but for the strength to remain unmoved.

### The Mining Quarter (The Depths)

The economic powerhouse of Hawksreach extends deep into the mountain's heart. This district is a complex network of:

- The Grand Mineshafts - Massive tunnels reinforced with revolutionary engineering techniques, descending hundreds of feet into the mountain.
- The Forge District - Where raw ore becomes refined metal, featuring dozens of smithies and workshops.
- The Workers' Warren - Dense residential areas built into the cave systems, featuring efficient use of space and clever ventilation systems.
- The Engineers' Exchange - Where new mining techniques and technologies are developed and tested.
- The First Forge - Sebastian's original smithy, maintained as a shrine to craftsmanship and innovation.
- The mining Quarter also contains the three [Forging Families of Hawksreach](../../Non%20Player%20Characters%20(NPCs)/Forging%20Families%20of%20Hawksreach%201dac7339ad0580f4aba8cd0baad989f1.md) - each family responsible for arming the corvid corps or the royal family themselves

### The Merchant's Ring (The Belt)

A massive circular district that wraps around the mountain's natural concave, featuring:

- The Grand Market - A three-tiered marketplace connected by mechanical lifts, where goods from throughout the empire are traded.
- The Metalweaver's Guild Complex - A sprawling facility where master craftsmen create both practical and artistic metalwork.
- The Bronze Hammer Tavern - A famous establishment known for its three-story central hall and traditional [Orcish brewing technique](Hawksreach/Orcish%20brewing%20technique%201dbc7339ad0580fc99c5f9142fc06352.md) .
- The Scorched Gate - The partially melted remains of the original city entrance, preserved as a monument to survival against a dragon attack.
- The Artificers' Arcade - A street of shops specialising in magical items and enhanced equipment.

### The Scholar's Terrace (The Mind)

An extensive district dedicated to learning and preservation of knowledge, featuring:

- The Great Library of Hawksreach - Housing historical records, technical manuscripts, and magical tomes.
- The School of Metallurgy and Engineering - Where the empire's brightest minds develop new technologies.
- The Hall of Memories - A museum dedicated to the city's history, centered around the massive statue of Ultark.

## City Secrets

Several mysteries lurk beneath Hawksreach's surface:

- The Forgotten Levels - Beneath the lowest mining levels, explorers have reported finding evidence of an ancient civilization that possessed advanced metallurgical knowledge. The Royal Guard maintains heavy restrictions on access to these areas.
- The Corvid Bond - The true nature of the relationship between the Corvids and the city remains mysterious. Some theorise that the birds are connected to an ancient ley line network that converges beneath the city.
- The Shadow Market - Rumors persist of a hidden marketplace in the old mining tunnels where rare artifacts and forbidden knowledge are traded.
- The Singing Caves - Deep in the mountain, miners speak of caverns where the rocks themselves seem to hum with an unexplained resonance. Some believe these vibrations are connected to the city's exceptional metalworking capabilities.
- The Vault of Mirrors - A sealed chamber discovered during recent mining operations, said to contain perfectly polished metallic surfaces that show reflections of possible futures. Access is strictly controlled by the royal family. Lady of Pain related?

The city maintains numerous defensive secrets as well, including hidden escape routes, emergency water reserves, and a network of mechanical warning systems integrated throughout the mountain.
