# Heart of Karthax

**Description:**

A crystallized titan's heart the size of a wine barrel, suspended within an ornate brass and platinum framework that stands 8 feet tall. The heart itself appears to be made of dark crimson stone shot through with veins of pulsing golden light, still beating once every few minutes with a deep resonance that can be felt in one's chest rather than heard. The framework is covered in Dawn War era glyphs and features twelve brass arms that hold focusing lenses aimed at the heart's surface. The entire assembly weighs nearly 1,000 pounds and requires at least four people to move it, though it can be carefully secured to a vehicle. Motes of golden light occasionally drift upward from the heart like embers.

**Connection to the Seal Codex:**
The heart belonged to Karthax the Delver, a titan who sided with the primordials during the Dawn War. As the gods gained the upper hand, Karthax—in a final act of spite—plunged himself deep into the earth, attempting to tunnel into the Far Realm and release its horrors upon the victorious gods. His body became lodged in the barrier between worlds, creating the arcane scar beneath Gravenhollows. While the gods slew Karthax, they could not fully remove his corpse without tearing the barrier completely. His petrified remains still serve as an anchor point where reality is thin.

The heart is a leftover from that - it washed up on the other side of the world centuries later, and eventually was bound and protected within the Isle of Cardiax. 

**Why the Clerics Protect It:**
The Isle of Cardiax guards this relic for multiple reasons. First, destroying the heart might sever the metaphysical connection and blind the Seal Codex to new breaches.
