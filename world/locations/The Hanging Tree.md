# The Hanging Tree

[Dohma Raskovar](../Non%20Player%20Characters%20(NPCs)/Dohma%20Raskovar%201efc7339ad0580ae9c60f2f7f395a7ab.md) runs the throne from the Hanging Tree, his tavern where he plays the role of barkeep. Here, clients in the know can discreetly indicate their desire to hire bounty hunters, cutthroats, or thieves by ordering a boiling oil, a mundane blend of whiskey and rum that appears on no menu. Once the drink is ordered, Raskovar himself appears at the table, ready to do business. He prides himself on remaining politically neutral and treating all potential customers as equals, something that chafes a few of his clients.

The Hanging Tree stands as a testament to nature's grandeur transformed by mortal hands. Built within the hollowed heart of an ancient tree in a secluded glade on Hua Shan's middle tier, this remarkable tavern draws its name from the grim decoration adorning its interior - countless weapons suspended from its ceiling, their blades glinting in the lamplight like metallic stalactites.

The tree itself, still living despite its modified state, spreads its vast canopy over the glade. Its trunk, wider than several men standing shoulder to shoulder, has been skillfully hollowed to house the tavern's main chamber. A domed roof, crafted to complement the tree's natural architecture, caps the structure, providing shelter while maintaining the organic aesthetic of this unique establishment. At a glance, the roof looks like a well trimmed tree, but upon closer inspection the leaves are made of pieces of finely decorated slate woven into and attached to each other.

The body of the building itself is made of the hollowed remains of the tree, with a larger extended part at the back of the tavern that has been build on separately. 

The ceiling in the tavern is 15 feet high. Old and broken blades are wedged between the ceiling stones. If a creature attacks the ceiling, the blades fall directly down in a 10-foot-diameter cylinder centered on the point that was attacked. If this debris passes through a creature's space, they must succeed on a DC 12 Dexterity saving throw or take 5 (2d4) slashing damage. Each area's ceiling can only drop blades once.

**Illumination.** Waist-high ivory lanterns stand in the corners of every area and fill the place with bright white light. All lights can simultaneously be turned off, turned red, or turned back to white by using an action to speak aloud a command word known to all members of the Ivory Throne: nict (dark), orr (red), and ior (white). Turning the lights red alerts the entire complex to intruders (see ["Ivory Throne Mercenaries"](https://5e.tools/adventure.html#HangingTree,0,ivory%20throne%20mercenaries,0)) and prompts Dohma Raskovar to use the magic shaker in the tavern to teleport to the Ivory Throne

# The Ivory Throne

Hidden beneath the Hanging Tree tavern is the true headquarters of the Ivory Throne, a base with deadly defensive measures and lavish ornamentation. Here, Dohma Raskovar plots new jobs with his mercenary band and stores his treasures.
