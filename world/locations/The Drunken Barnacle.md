# The Drunken Barnacle

**The Drunken Barnacle**

Nestled on a sturdy stone platform in one of the older districts, the Drunken Barnacle is a popular haunt for sailors, fishermen, merchants, and even the occasional off-duty warrior, pirate or soldier. 

- It's strategically located near one of the larger docking areas, making it a natural first (or last) stop for those arriving or departing by sea. The top of the roof holds a huge barnacle, ~30-40ft high.
- There is a fisherman’s pole near the entrance to the tavern.
- The tavern is three stories, built into the side of Hua Shan. The rooms (of which the tavern has many) are on the third floor, with 4 corridors of ~10-12 rooms each.

**Interiors:**

- **Lantern Light and Shadow:** Light spills from numerous paper lanterns hanging from the low, beamed ceiling, casting dancing shadows across the rough-hewn wooden floor. Some lanterns are adorned with hand-painted scenes of ships battling mythical beasts or serene coastal landscapes.
- **The Barnacle-Encrusted Bar:** The centerpiece of the tavern is a long, curved bar made from a single, thick piece of driftwood, its surface smoothed by countless spilled drinks and leaned-upon elbows. barnacles, long since petrified, still cling to its sides. Behind the bar, shelves groan under the weight of mismatched ceramic and glass bottles filled with local rice wines, potent spirits distilled from fermented fruits, and the surprisingly popular (and slightly salty) Durgam sea ale (1sp/drink). Sea stew is usually served, either with rice or bread,
- **Seating and Nooks:** Patrons can perch on wobbly wooden stools pulled up to the bar or gather around a collection of mismatched tables and benches scattered throughout the room. Several smaller, curtained alcoves offer a modicum of privacy when they are free. One corner features a raised platform where a musician might occasionally strum an instrument or a storyteller captivates the crowd with tales of the sea.
- **A Glimpse Below:** Just past the doorway, a reinforced glass panel offers a view into the shallow waters below, where colourful fish dart amongst the supporting stilts.
