# The Isle of Cardiax

(current in game date: ~late sprouting 333AC).

### Background

Over the past few months, the evil clerics of Abbey Isle have quarreled violently with a large band of local pirates (see [Ivory Throne (mercenary band)](../Non%20Player%20Characters%20(NPCs)/Ivory%20Throne%20(mercenary%20band)%201efc7339ad05804aad72eaded63fa687.md)). Their struggle seems to have left the island battlescarred. The pirates burned a lot of the abbey to the ground, but they suffered a significant number of casualties afterwards even though their mission was successful.

Unknown to the ivory throne, a small number of clerics survived the assaults by taking refuge in the cellars of the ruined abbey. Hidden halls, dangerous guardians, and shining treasures await those who dare to explore the depths of the abbey, as well as information about dealings nefarious to the state of the wider world.

### Daily Life and Rituals

Life on the Isle of Cardiax is structured around the heart's rhythm. Every 3-4 minutes when the heart beats, bells toll throughout the monastery complex. Wardens stop whatever they're doing to speak a brief prayer of binding in unison. This happens roughly 400 times per day. The constant interruption makes normal conversation nearly impossible, so the Wardens have developed an elaborate sign language for daily communication.

The isle itself is austere—stone buildings, minimal decoration, and an atmosphere of perpetual tension. Wardens sleep in shifts, with at least a dozen always attending the Reliquary chamber. They eat communally in silence, study the Seal Codex entries obsessively to understand what the heart reveals, and train rigorously in both divine magic and combat, knowing that cultists and primordial worshippers constantly seek to steal the heart.
