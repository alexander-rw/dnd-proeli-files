Rules:

- Flavour is free
- If you aren’t enjoying an aspect of the game, tell me, I will happily work out how we can change that
- Full long rests only occur in safe places; during travel through the wilderness, or during dungeons, you might only be able to partially long rest.
- Two rolls for the same check, unless I ask everyone to make one. This represents the party’s best go at something
    - If you’ve got another idea for the same check, tell me, i’ll let you know if you can roll that as a third - usually, it’ll be no, but sometimes I may allow it with disadvantage
- You need proficiency to help someone else out with a roll. Otherwise, you can make the roll yourself (provided there haven’t been two total rolls other than yours)
- Roleplay! if there’s anything *I* can do to make you feel more comfortable roleplaying, then please let me know.
- Those of you that are spellcasters:
    - don’t forget about your material components. Look at your spells carefully, some spells require them and depending on where you are, the components may be scarce. New spells don’t automatically give you the components!
- Spells do *only* what they say; no more, no less.
- Divisive topics (racism, sexism, slavery)
- If you speak in character, you’re the one asking the question
- Keep combat quick where you can
- Partial long rests give you back: 1/3 of your hit die, rounded down, and a number of spell slots equal to 1d6, distributed how you want. Class features reset during partial long rests equal to 1/2 of your prof bonus, rounded up, tbd if this rule doesn’t quite work

## Spells

- You’re free to use spells from other official sources, with the caveat that I might change the spell if I find it unfun to DM against.
- If you are a spellcaster, I’ll work with you to choose your spells when you level up (or make you aware of them if you prepare spells).
- I use the PHB14 version of Contagion for NPCs (you are welcome to use this too)

## Conditions

A condition is a temporary game state. The definition of a condition says how it affects its recipient, and various rules define how to end a condition. 

I add the following condition(s) to our game:

- Dazed
    - An affected target's Speed is halved
    - it can't take Reactions
    - On its turns, it can take either an Action or a Bonus Action, not both
    - If it takes the Attack action, it can make only one attack
    - If it casts a spell with a Somatic component, there is a 25 percent chance the spell fails as a result of the target making the spell's gestures too slowly. (roll a d4; on a 1, the spell fails)

## Exhaustive spell casting

- If you are out of spell slots, you can cast a spell by taking exhaustion levels up equal to 2 + the level of the spell being cast.
- You cast the spell at the lowest level of the spell, and not higher, even by taking further levels of exhaustion
- Abilities that are not spells cause an exhaustion level equal to the level at which you gain the corresponding feature
- This also interacts with the resurrection rules: as this represents pushing past your limits, resurrection magic will be harder after you do this. rules TBD
